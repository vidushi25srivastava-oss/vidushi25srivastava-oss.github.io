Website Content Transformation — 3 Pages to Full Product Ecosystem
DigiStay.ai · Sept 2025 – Present
The Problem

DigiStay launched with a minimal 3-page website that couldn't communicate the full scope of its AI-powered hotel management platform. Product value was buried in feature lists rather than user outcomes, and the site lacked the depth and structure needed to support a multi-stakeholder buyer journey — from independent hotel owners to enterprise resort chains.

There was no content system, no consistent messaging hierarchy, and no pages to support the product's expanding feature set — including digital check-in, channel manager, booking engine, and AI tools.

My Role & Approach

End-to-end ownership: content strategy, UX copy, information architecture, Figma execution, and performance monitoring.

I began by mapping the user decision journey — understanding what different buyer segments (boutique owners, resort managers, chain operators) needed to see at each stage of evaluation. From this, I built a multi-page content architecture covering all core product features with consistent messaging across every page.

The central strategic shift was from feature-focused to benefit-led communication — every section was reframed around what the product does for the hotelier, not just what it does.

The AI Operating System for Modern Hospitality
DigiStay runs on AI agents to manage operations, guest experience, and revenue — so you don't have to. Your AI Copilot doesn't just assist, it runs your hotel.

Product Page Copy UX Writing Conversion Copywriting
Digital Check-In Product Page — From Feature List to Guest-First Narrative
DigiStay.ai · Hotel Digital Check-In Solution
Context & Objective

The Digital Check-In page needed to speak to two distinct audiences simultaneously: hotel owners evaluating an operational tool, and hotel guests experiencing a new arrival process. Both needed to feel confident and reassured — but with very different concerns.

The page objective was to demonstrate that DigiStay's check-in software reduces operational burden for staff while creating a premium, frictionless experience for guests — and to drive demo bookings from property owners.

Content Structure & Decisions

I structured the page around three content arcs: (1) guest benefit (what guests feel), (2) operational benefit (what staff experience), and (3) technical trust (security and compliance assurance).

The UX copy for the step-by-step process (Select nationality → Upload ID → Enter details → Pay → Check-in) was written to feel as simple and linear as possible — eliminating jargon at every step.

A "Web Check-In" section was added to handle the pre-arrival flow, using structured parallel copy to walk through the process for both the guest and the hotel.

Copy Sample — Hero Headline & Subhead
Turn Waiting into Welcome with Digital Check-In
Skip the queue with DigiStay's AI-powered digital check-in. Reduce waiting time by 70% and offer a seamless, contactless arrival experience. The top hotel check-in software lets guests have a calm, seamless, and stress-free arrival.
Why this works: "Turn Waiting into Welcome" is a benefit contrast (negative state → positive outcome) compressed into 5 words. The 70% metric anchors the promise in a specific, credible number. "Calm, seamless, stress-free" targets the emotional state of the guest — not the features of the software. The word "calm" is deliberate: hotels selling luxury sell feelings, not tools.

Copy Sample — Benefits Section (UX Microcopy)
Check-In Smarter: Benefits for Hotels and Guests
Turn hectic check-ins into effortless first impressions.

The AI security section ("All IDs are stored securely for up to 5 years") is written to build legal confidence, not just feature coverage.

Landing Page SEO Copywriting Content Strategy
Channel Manager Landing Page — SEO + Conversion Hybrid
DigiStay.ai · Channel Manager Solution
Context & Objective

The Channel Manager page had a dual mandate: perform organically for competitive hotel tech keywords while converting hotelier visitors into demo bookings. This required balancing SEO depth (long-form educational content) with sharp conversion copy and a clear CTA structure.

The target audience was hotel owners and revenue managers who may already know what a channel manager is — the copy couldn't over-explain basics, but needed to clearly differentiate DigiStay from established competitors.

Strategy & Copy Decisions

The page was structured as a problem-solution-proof flow: start with the pain (overbookings, manual updates, revenue loss), introduce the solution (real-time sync, dynamic pricing), then validate with social proof and pricing clarity.

An educational "What is a Channel Manager?" section was included for SEO depth, but written in plain language — avoiding the jargon-heavy tone typical of hotel tech companies. This also served to build trust with less digitally-sophisticated buyers.

Dynamic pricing content was given dedicated real estate, with a data visualisation element ("+24% Revenue This month") to create an aspirational reference point.

Copy Sample — Hero
Stop Losing Bookings. Meet Your Hotel Channel Manager.
One Simple Solution to Manage All Your Booking Websites. Thousands of hotels trust DigiStay to prevent overbookings, optimize rates, and maximize revenue — all in a few simple steps.
Why this works: The hero opens with a loss-aversion hook ("Stop Losing") — one of the highest-converting frames for B2B SaaS. The period after the first sentence creates a dramatic pause before the product name. "One Simple Solution" counters the complexity fear that hotel owners have toward tech tools. The social proof ("Thousands of hotels trust") is embedded in the hero, not relegated to a lower section — building confidence early in the scroll journey.

Copy Sample — Feature Section (Benefit-Led)
Why Do You Need a Channel Manager?
Managing multiple hotel booking channels can be overwhelming and prone to errors. A channel manager helps hotels stay organised by preventing overbookings, automatically updating rates, and maintaining consistent pricing across platforms.
Copy decisions: Each feature is written as a micro benefit statement: "Prevent overbookings across OTAs — Automatic inventory sync prevents double bookings & protects your reputation." The "& protects your reputation" tail is strategic — it connects a technical feature to a business identity concern (reputation), which resonates more strongly with independent hotel owners than operational metrics alone.

Conversion Copywriting Landing Page Messaging Strategy
Booking Engine Page — Reframing the Direct Booking Argument
DigiStay.ai · Hotel Booking Engine Solution
Context & Objective

Most Indian hotels are deeply reliant on OTA platforms like MakeMyTrip and Booking.com, often paying 15–20% commissions per booking. The Booking Engine page needed to convince skeptical, OTA-dependent hoteliers that switching to direct bookings was not just possible, but financially urgent.

The challenge was overcoming both inertia ("It's working for us now") and fear ("What if direct bookings don't come?"). The copy had to be financially persuasive, not just feature-descriptive.

Messaging Strategy

I built the page around a financial loss framing: every booking through an OTA is money leaving the business. This reframed the product purchase as a cost-saving decision, not a tech adoption decision — lowering psychological friction.

An "OTA vs DigiStay" comparison section was created to visualize the contrast directly. Data points like "20% commission saved per booking" and "97% payment success rate" were placed near CTAs to reinforce trust at the moment of decision.

The "One Change. Immediate Impact." section was written as an urgency device — quantifying the cost of delay rather than just listing benefits.

Copy Sample — Hero
Stop Losing On Commissions — Get More Direct Bookings With Our Hotel Booking Engine
DigiStay's hotel booking engine powers direct bookings for every kind of property — from hotels and resorts to homestays, hostels, and villas. Give your guests a secure, seamless, and branded booking experience, no matter where they stay.
Why this works: "Stop Losing" is a deliberate loss-aversion frame — stronger than "Start Earning More." The em-dash mid-headline creates a cause-effect relationship: lose commissions → gain direct bookings. The subhead expands scope immediately ("every kind of property") to prevent self-exclusion by smaller property types. "Branded booking experience" subtly addresses OTA brand-washing — a real pain point for hospitality businesses trying to build guest loyalty.

Copy Sample — Pain Point Section
Challenges Hotels Face Without a Booking Engine
Up to 20% commission is gone per booking. Limited brand visibility — guests remember the OTA, not your property. Payment delays hamper cash flow.
Copy decisions: This section deliberately names the pain without softening it — "commission is gone" (not "fees apply"), "guests remember the OTA, not your property" (an identity and brand threat, not just a revenue metric). Each pain point ends with a business consequence, not just an operational description. This structure mirrors the "problem agitation" technique — making the reader feel the cost of inaction before introducing the solution.

UX Writing Product Messaging Pricing Page Copy
AI Product Messaging & Pricing Architecture
DigiStay.ai · Homepage AI Section + Pricing Page
Context & Objective

As DigiStay evolved into a full AI platform (AI Copilot, AI Receptionist, AI Revenue Manager), the core homepage messaging needed to reflect this shift without alienating non-technical hoteliers who associate "AI" with complexity and job replacement.

Simultaneously, the pricing page needed to clearly segment four buyer tiers — from independent stays to enterprise groups — with tier names and feature lists that would make self-selection easy and frictionless.

Copy Strategy

For the AI section, I wrote each AI product with a distinct role identity: Copilot (operations), Receptionist (guest communication), Revenue Manager (pricing). This gave non-technical buyers a human frame of reference — these are roles they already understand, now automated.

The section header — "Meet the AI That Runs Your Hotel" — uses second-person ownership language. "Your hotel" makes the AI feel personally useful rather than generic.

For the pricing page, tier names were selected to map to buyer identity: "For Independent Stays," "For Growing Hotels, Chains," "For Groups, Premium Properties," "For Multiple Properties." Each label is a mirror, not a marketing claim.

Copy Sample — AI Section (Homepage)
Everything Your Hotel Needs, Automated by AI
Traditional hotel software gives you tools. DigiStay gives you intelligence. At the core of DigiStay is a powerful AI layer that manages your entire hospitality ecosystem — from operations to revenue to guest experience.
Why this works: The contrast sentence ("tools vs intelligence") is a classic positioning frame that repositions all competitors as feature-sets rather than solutions. "Ecosystem" elevates scope — this isn't a point tool, it's an operating layer. The three pillars (operations, revenue, guest experience) in the final sentence mirror a hotelier's three core performance areas, making the claim feel complete and comprehensive without listing every feature.

Copy Sample — Pricing Tier Labels & Architecture
Plans That Fit Every Hotel
Flexible pricing for hotels of all sizes. Starting at just ₹1000/month — choose the plan that fits your hotel.
Copy decisions: Tier subtitles ("For Independent Stays," "For Growing Hotels, Chains") use the buyer's self-description — not abstract tier language like "Starter" or "Pro." The pricing anchor ("Starting at just ₹1000/month") appears on product pages before users reach the pricing page, reducing sticker shock at the decision stage. The enterprise tier uses "Contact Sales" with specific enterprise features listed to set expectations and qualify leads without blocking exploration.
